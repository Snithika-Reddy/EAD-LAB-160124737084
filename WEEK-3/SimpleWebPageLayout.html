The Mahabharata is not just the story of a war — it is the story of life itself. Written in Sanskrit by Sage Ved Vyasa more than 5,000 years ago, this epic contains over one lakh shlokas, making it the longest poem in the world. Yet its true greatness lies not in its length but in its lessons. At the centre of the Mahabharata is the struggle between the Pandavas and the Kauravas for the throne of Hastinapur. But beyond the battles, it is about choices, values, friendships, and duties that still matter today. It teaches what happens when greed and jealousy take over, and how truth and righteousness always find a way to shine.
